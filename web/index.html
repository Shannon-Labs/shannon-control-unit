Project 2

Driftlock Choir

Wireless Synchronization via Chronometric Interferometry.

Figure: The "Beat Note" interference pattern allowing picosecond-level locking.

The Concept

How do you synchronize a swarm of drones or a distributed antenna array without wires? GPS is too imprecise (nanoseconds). Atomic clocks are too expensive.

The Invention

Chronometric Interferometry. By measuring the interference beat note between two drifting oscillators, we can mathematically reconstruct the exact time offset.

It's like a choir breathing in unison. Even if individual singers drift, the collective interference creates a lock.
